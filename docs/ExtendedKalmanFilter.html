Extended Kalman Filter

In cases where the belief cannot be always expressed as a linear transformation of a Gaussian random variable, rendering the posterior distribution impossible to calculate by normal means, a solution exists: those non-linear transformations are linearized with a first-order Taylor expansion centered at the mean of the previous Gaussian. This allows for the final distribution to be of a Gaussian shape again allowing the algorithm to work again. While this approach is much more efficient compared to the Monte Carlo method used to calculate the non-Gaussian posterior, it also introduces its own error to the final mean and variance. Just like KF it is unable to work with multimodal distributions.
